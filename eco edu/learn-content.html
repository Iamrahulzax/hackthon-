What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These shifts may be natural, but since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas.

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. When the Sun's energy reaches the Earth's atmosphere, some of it is reflected back to space and the rest is absorbed and re-radiated by greenhouse gases.

Greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, keeping Earth warm enough to support life. Without the greenhouse effect, Earth would be too cold for life as we know it.

Effects of Climate Change

Climate change is already having visible effects on the world:

  • Higher temperatures across the globe
  • More extreme weather events like heatwaves and heavy rainfall
  • Rising sea levels due to melting ice and thermal expansion of seawater
  • Changes in wildlife populations and habitats
  • Impact on agriculture and food production

What Can We Do?

While climate change is a global challenge, individuals can make a difference through:

  • Reducing energy consumption
  • Using renewable energy sources
  • Supporting sustainable transportation
  • Reducing waste and recycling
  • Advocating for climate-friendly policies

Every action counts in the fight against climate change. Start with small changes in your daily life, and encourage others to do the same.
